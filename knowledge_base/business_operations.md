# Business Operations Knowledge Base

This document describes how the business operates across departments and months, and how key operational metrics should be interpreted and used for decision-making.  
It is designed to support analytical agents performing optimization, validation, and recommendation tasks.

---

## Business Structure

The business operates across multiple **departments**, such as:
- Core
- SaaS
- AI Products

Each department is evaluated **monthly** using operational, financial, and delivery-related metrics.

---

## Dataset Overview

Each row in the dataset represents the performance of one department in one month.

### Columns and Meaning

- **department**  
  The business unit responsible for generating revenue and delivering products or services.

- **month**  
  The reporting period (integer, sequential).

- **revenue**  
  Total monthly revenue generated by the department.  
  Higher revenue generally indicates stronger demand or pricing power.

- **hr_spend**  
  Cost related to human resources (salaries, contractors, benefits).  
  High HR spend may indicate growth, complexity, or inefficiency.

- **operational_cost**  
  Non-HR operating expenses (infrastructure, tooling, cloud, logistics).  
  This is a key target for cost optimization initiatives.

- **product_margin**  
  Revenue minus direct costs related to delivering the product.  
  Higher margins indicate healthier unit economics.

- **active_customers**  
  Number of customers actively using or paying for the product.  
  Changes may signal growth, churn, or delivery issues.

- **delivery_risk**  
  Categorical risk indicator:
  - 0 = Low risk
  - 1 = Medium risk
  - 2 = High risk  
  This reflects the likelihood of delays, outages, or SLA breaches.

---

## Operational Interpretation Guidelines

### Cost Efficiency
- High **operational_cost** relative to **revenue** suggests infrastructure or process inefficiencies.
- High **hr_spend** without corresponding revenue growth may indicate overstaffing or low productivity.

### Profitability
- **Product margin** is a primary indicator of financial health.
- Declining margins over time should trigger investigation into costs or pricing.

### Customer Impact
- A drop in **active_customers** may correlate with:
  - Increased delivery risk
  - Reduced product quality
  - Operational instability

### Delivery Risk Management
- High **delivery_risk** often correlates with:
  - High operational cost
  - Rapid scaling without adequate infrastructure
  - Understaffed or overstretched teams
- Reducing delivery risk typically improves customer retention and long-term revenue stability.

---

## Common Optimization Strategies

### Reduce Operational Cost
- Identify months where operational_cost grows faster than revenue.
- Prioritize cost reductions that do not increase delivery risk.

### Improve Margins
- Focus on departments with strong revenue but low product margins.
- Optimize infrastructure, automation, or supplier contracts.

### Balance HR Spend
- Ensure HR spend aligns with revenue growth and customer demand.
- Avoid aggressive cost-cutting that increases delivery risk.

### Monitor Risk Trends
- Track delivery_risk trends over time per department.
- Prevent prolonged high-risk periods, even if short-term revenue is strong.

---

## Decision-Making Principles for Agents

When making recommendations:
- Prefer actions that improve margins without increasing delivery risk.
- Avoid cost reductions that significantly reduce active customers.
- Flag departments with sustained high costs and low margins.
- Use historical trends rather than single-month anomalies.

---

## Summary

This business operates in a multi-department, monthly cycle where:
- Revenue growth must be balanced against HR and operational costs.
- Product margin is the key profitability signal.
- Delivery risk is critical for customer trust and long-term stability.

Agents should use this knowledge to make grounded, data-driven operational recommendations.
